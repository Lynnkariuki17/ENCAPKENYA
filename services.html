Bonds & Bills

Bonds are financial securities that represent a loan made by an investor to an entity. Bonds are considered to be fixed income investments because they offer a predictable stream of income and a relatively stable return of capital. Bonds can be categorized into Eurbonds and local treasury bonds. Investing in Bonds provide income and is a a lower risk alternative.

Stock Trading

Stock Trading involves buying and selling of shares of publicly traded companies on stock exchanges at the Nairobi Stock Exchange or Global stock exchanges. The primary goal of stock trading is to generate returns by buying shares at a lower price and selling them at a higher price, or by receiving dividends from underlying companies.

Global Funds

These are financial instruments that invest the funds out of Kenya for greener pastures, security or for an exposure in the world markets like USA and India. We provide clients with th necessary platforms and assist them to choose the Stocks/ETF's.

Derivatives

Derivatives are financial instruments whose value is derived from the value of an underlying asset, index or rate. Rather than having intrinsic value on their own they are used to hedge against price fluctuations or to speculate price movements in the underlying asset. They include Futures and options.

Commodities

Commodities are physical goods that are bought and sold in large quantities on organized markets these include agricultural products and non agricultural like gold and oil. By investing in commodities, investors can potentially benefit from price fluctuations in the underlying markets as well as the growth and success of underlying industries. Commodities include Gold, Silver, Cotton, Sugar etc.
